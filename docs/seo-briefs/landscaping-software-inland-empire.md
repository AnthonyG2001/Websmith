## 2. Search intent classification
- **Primary intent:** Commercial (0.579 commercial + 0.31 informational on the search-intent endpoint). DFS labs concurs `main_intent: commercial`.
- **User goal in plain English:** A landscaping business owner is researching SEO agencies — likely heading into or out of peak season (March-October is landscaping's busy period in the IE; SEO inquiries cluster Jan-March as owners plan for the season and Sep-Nov as they reflect on the season just finished). Landscapers are operationally sophisticated about lead quality vs. lead volume — they distinguish *maintenance* (recurring revenue) from *design-build* (large one-shot) and need to know which we're optimizing for.
- **Decision-stage:** Consideration (mid-funnel) — vetting *who*, with sub-decision of whether to weight SEO toward maintenance keywords or design-build keywords.
- **Implication:** Page must acknowledge the maintenance-vs-design-build distinction in service-card copy. Lead with seasonality framing similar to Roofing but reversed (landscaping = spring/summer-peaked, roofing = fall/winter-peaked).

**What to borrow:**
- The **maintenance-vs-design-build / commercial-vs-residential** segmentation framing — Bullberry skews commercial-only, which is a valid niche. We acknowledge both.
- LSI keyword pattern is correct: "landscape maintenance services," "landscape irrigation systems," "landscape design-build," "lawn care," "tree trimming," "hardscape installation" — these are real GBP-adjacent categories. We use them tightly.
- The **state-level scope (`/services/state/california`) URL pattern** is a programmatic-page architecture — interesting but out of scope for us (we're IE-only).

**What to NOT do:**
- The page is **the most bloated competitor we've parsed** at ~3,500+ words — most of it is template repetition with LSI-keyword stuffing. Hard "no" on this pattern.

- **"30 years of experience"** mentioned in the org-branding — a credibility play we can't match. Don't try to. Compete on operational specificity and IE-local instead.
- The page **doesn't name any actual schema markup, GBP categories, or specific competitor analysis** — it's all generic. Our specificity wins.
- Bullberry runs **separate subdomains per vertical** (`landscaping.bullberry.com`, `roofing.bullberry.com`, etc.) — clever architecture but we use single-domain with industry slugs (`/Landscaping.html`, `/Roofing.html`).
