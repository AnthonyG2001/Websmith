## 2. Search intent classification
- **Primary intent:** Commercial (0.776 on the search-intent endpoint for `pool service marketing`). DFS labs main_intent: commercial. Cleanly commercial — no foreign intent muddying the read.
- **User goal in plain English:** A pool service company owner (typically a 1-3 truck route operator) is searching for marketing help. Pool service has **seasonal demand** (peak May-August in the IE — when temps cross 90°F and homeowners can't ignore green water any longer) and **recurring-revenue route economics** (each new monthly customer is worth $50-200/mo on autopay for years if you don't churn them). Owners value lead quality over volume because servicing a route customer who quits in 3 months loses money on the windshield time.
- **Decision-stage:** Consideration. The buyer has decided to spend on marketing; they're comparing approaches (Google Ads vs. SEO vs. door hangers vs. Nextdoor).
- **Implication:** Page must speak to **route density and seasonal cadence**, not just "we'll rank you." The CPC of $37.06 is telling — Google Ads bidders are spending heavily, which means SEO is genuinely the cheaper long-game alternative we're offering.

## 4. Competing pages to learn from

**Shared parsing source for S3:** `outpaceseo.com/services/seo-for-cleaning-business/` (parsed once for the whole 4-brief session — see Cleaning brief §4 for the full read).

**What the parse pattern tells us applies to Pool too:**
- Outpace SEO's page is ~1,500 words but **almost entirely generic SEO-agency boilerplate** (audit, on-page, technical, schema, local SEO, authority) with thin vertical specificity. Their pool/garage-door/chimney pages will follow the same template — same headings, same testimonials, vertical-keyword swapped.
- **Testimonials shown on the page come from unrelated industries** (Statewide Health Clinic, Foot & Ankle Surgeon, Vape e-commerce, Rehab Center) — not pool, not cleaning. The page presents social proof that doesn't actually match the vertical.
- The cluster of look-alike low-quality SEO sites (the entire `redlandschimneysweep.org/.us/.com` web visible in the Chimney SERP, and PoolTexx's "#1 weekly pool service" claim) tells us **the bar is shockingly low**. Operational specificity wins.

**For the Pool page specifically, what to borrow / avoid:**
- **Borrow:** Outpace's section on Local SEO Domination correctly points out that cleaning is a recurring service — same is true for pool service routes. Frame route customers as long-term revenue, not one-off jobs.
- **Avoid:** Generic "we do audit + on-page + content + local SEO" lists. Frontend must write tighter and lead with **pool-specific operations** — route density, seasonal cadence, the green-water emergency pattern, solar pool heating cross-sell, salt-cell replacement, automation upgrades.
- **Avoid:** Numbered hero stats like "Organic Leads YoY" without backing data (Outpace shows these as floating text without numbers). Our commit/disclaim band is the antidote.
- **Avoid:** Claims like "ranks in the top 3 for the majority of our keywords" — we never copy this language.
