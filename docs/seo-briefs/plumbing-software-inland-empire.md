## 2. Search intent classification
- **Primary intent:** Commercial (0.462 commercial, 0.431 informational — buyer is researching SEO agencies AND learning how SEO for plumbing works in the same session).
- **User goal in plain English:** A plumbing business owner (or office manager) is investigating whether to hire an SEO agency. They want proof of plumbing-specific expertise AND they want to understand what the engagement looks like before they call.
- **Decision-stage:** Consideration (mid-funnel) — they know they need *something*, they're vetting *who*. They are NOT ready to buy on landing; they are ready to vet.
- **Implication:** Page must teach (informational coverage) AND prove (commercial credibility) on the same scroll. Heavy CTA above the fold would over-rotate to transactional intent and lose the researcher.

**What to NOT do:**

- Vague generalities ("most homeowners search within a 10-15 mile radius") with no source. We **cite our LF scan numbers** instead — defensible, novel, on-page proof.
- Pseudo-quote from "Redlands Business Owner" with no name. We do not use fake testimonials per §11.
- ZIP-code drops ("92373 and 92374") feel like SEO stuffing without context. We mention zips in passing only, in service of usefulness.
