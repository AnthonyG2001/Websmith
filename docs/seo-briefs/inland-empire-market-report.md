### §1 — How we collected the data

Between May 12 and May 13, 2026, we ran 5×5 geographic grid scans across 20 Inland Empire and LA County–adjacent cities using [Local Falcon](https://www.localfalcon.com/), the standard tool for measuring share of local voice in Google Maps results.

Each scan: 5×5 grid (25 measurement points per city), 2-mile radius from city center, search keyword `local seo [city]`. Total measurement points: 500 city grid points + 25 industry-vertical points = **525 individual SERP measurements**.

We also ran one industry-vertical comparison scan for `plumbing seo redlands` (§4).

Every number in this report traces to a specific Local Falcon report key, listed in §11.

**What this report measures:** unique competitor count, active SoLV competitors, leader name + SoLV%, leader Google review count, average top-3 proximity distance.

**What this report does not measure (limitations):** single-snapshot data; single-keyword query (`local seo [city]` only); 2-mile radius; Maps results only; agency vertical only.

**Scan B — `[plumbing seo redlands](/Plumbing.html)`** (Google's NLP skews this pack toward actual service businesses):

| Rank | GBP | SoLV | Reviews |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Water Heater S.O.S. | 100% | **513** |
| 2 | Pride Plumbing | 88% | **917** |
| 3 | All Purpose Plumbing | 80% | **110** |

Same city, same 2-mile radius, same grid methodology. **Top 3 plumber review counts: 513, 917, 110.** Top 3 agency review counts: 3, 11, 8.

This is the central asymmetry of the IE local-SEO market:

- In **service-business verticals** (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing, garage-door), review velocity is the moat. The pack leader has hundreds of reviews; a small operator without that base cannot break in on relevance signals alone.
- In **agency-vs-agency competition**, review depth is almost entirely absent across the field. Pack leaders win on GBP category cleanliness, NAP consistency, on-page schema, and proximity.

Practical implications: **if your competitors run 100+ reviews, you cannot rank without matching the curve.** For the IE marketing agency, the field is wide open for anyone who runs a real review-request cadence.

**Notable runner-up:** In [Fontana](/Fontana.html), Internet Marketing Agency Fontana CA holds 32% SoLV (#3 position) with zero Google reviews — sitting behind a well-defended leader.

**Why this matters:** For any Inland Empire service business operating in one of these five cities, the Maps 3-pack for `local seo [city]` is held by an operator who has not built a defensive review base. The pack will not stay that way. Within 12 months, one of three outcomes:

1. The incumbent zero-review leader starts collecting reviews and converts the position from "held on relevance" to "defended by reviews."
2. A new entrant runs a 6-month review-request cadence and overtakes the position on prominence signal.
3. Google's algorithm shifts review weight upward and the zero-review leaders drop overnight.

Our money is on scenario 2 in San Bernardino and Moreno Valley (large population, real demand) and scenario 1 in Corona and Rialto (incumbents likely defend once they realize the position is exposed).

### §8 — Reading this report responsibly

**SoLV definition:** Share of local voice = percentage of grid points (out of 25) where a GBP appears in the local-pack ranking, weighted by position.

**Why `local seo [city]`?** Consistency across cities. Variations would produce a richer dataset but 80 separate scans instead of 20.

**Why grid 5×5 at 2 miles?** Local Falcon's default for small-to-mid-size cities. Industry-standard tradeoff.

**Snapshot date is load-bearing.** All numbers reflect local pack composition on May 12-13, 2026. Republished annually.

**Raw data:** report keys listed in §11. Email contact@softwarestrategists.com for verification.
