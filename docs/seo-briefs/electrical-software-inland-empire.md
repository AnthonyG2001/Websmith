### Related-keyword caveat
The `seo for electricians` related-keywords endpoint returned a high proportion of **adjacent-business-software keywords** (`electrical contractor software` 390/mo, `scheduling software for electricians` 140/mo, `housecall pro for electricians`, `best invoice app for electrical contractor`). The cluster's drift suggests Google is treating "SEO for electricians" as adjacent to "running an electrical business operationally," which means our copy needs to disambiguate quickly (Section 2 H2 makes this explicit).

## 2. Search intent classification
- **Primary intent:** Commercial (DFS labs) **AND** informational (search-intent endpoint 0.647). Vs. Plumbing/HVAC, electricians skew **more informational** — they're researching the *concept* of SEO more than vetting agencies. `electrical contractor marketing` is 71% commercial, but the umbrella `seo for electricians` is informational-led.
- **User goal in plain English:** An electrical contractor (often an owner-operator without an office manager — different from a multi-truck plumbing shop) is **learning what SEO even is for their trade** AND assessing whether an agency is worth it. Less ready-to-buy than the Plumbing/HVAC buyer.
- **Decision-stage:** Awareness → consideration (slightly earlier in the funnel than Plumbing/HVAC).
- **Implication:** Page must teach *more* and pitch *less* than the Plumbing page. We commit to slightly more body copy in the service-card lead-ins (explain why each pillar matters) and resist over-using "playbook" language that assumes the buyer already knows what SEO is. The hero CTA stays "Get the electrician playbook" but the trust strip and Section 2 H2 are pitched to teach.
