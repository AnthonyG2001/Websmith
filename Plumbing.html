[05]  QUESTIONS PLUMBERS ASK

Plumbing SEO, answered straight.

How do I promote my plumbing business online?
Three things, in order of impact: a fully optimized Google Business Profile with the right categories and service areas; a website Google can read (schema markup, fast LCP, one homeowner question per page); and a steady review-request cadence on every completed job. The plumbers ranking in our local geo-grid scans average 100 to 900 reviews — that's the bar.
How do plumbers get more customers from Google?
Most calls come from Google's Maps 3-pack, not the blue-link results below it. The 3-pack ranks on three signals: proximity to the searcher, relevance of your GBP categories and service areas, and prominence (reviews, citations, and on-site authority). Local SEO addresses all three. If you're not in the 3-pack for emergency plumbing keywords, you're effectively invisible to homeowners with a leak right now.
What is the best social media for plumbers?
Honest answer: for most Inland Empire plumbers, paid social is not the highest-ROI channel. Homeowners search Google when a pipe bursts — they don't scroll Instagram. We'd rather get your GBP and on-site SEO right first, then layer Facebook ads for water-heater replacements or repipe campaigns where the buyer has time to deliberate. Social is real, but it's not what fills the truck schedule.
How long does plumbing SEO take to work?
First measurable movement on Maps rankings typically shows in 30 to 60 days after GBP fixes and review cadence start. Organic-search ranking improvements on competitive keywords like 'plumber [city]' take 90 to 180 days because Google needs to crawl, re-evaluate, and re-index your pages. We re-run the geo-grid scan every quarter so you see the movement on the same map you started with.
