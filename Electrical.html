What ranking an electrical contractor actually takes

Electrical SEO isn't a content-marketing problem. It's a local-pack problem. Three things move that needle — done well, on schedule, every month.

  • GBP that wins the 3-pack. The right categories audited (Electrician / Electrical Installation Service / Lighting Contractor / Solar Energy Equipment Supplier where applicable — most IE electricians only claim one). Service area cleaned. Reviews requested after every panel upgrade, EV charger install, or emergency call. Posts that match what homeowners actually search — "EV charger installation cost," "panel upgrade 200 amp," not "electrical excellence."
  • A website Google can read. Schema markup for Electrician + Service + AggregateOffer where applicable. Service-area pages for the cities you actually drive to, not a fake nationwide footprint. Page speed under 2.5s LCP. One homeowner question per page — "do I need a permit for a panel upgrade?", not "comprehensive electrical solutions since 1995" filler that ranks for nothing.
  • Reviews & local citations on schedule. NAP consistency on the directories Google checks for electrical work (BBB, Angi, HomeAdvisor, IAEI, EnergySage for solar+EV electricians, manufacturer-locator pages for the panel brands you install). Review cadence that compounds — panel upgrades and EV installs convert into 5-stars at much higher rates than service calls because the homeowner just spent four figures and you didn't ruin their drywall.

[05]  QUESTIONS ELECTRICIANS ASK

Electrical SEO, answered straight.

What is electrician SEO?
Electrician SEO is the work of editing and creating website content and Google Business Profile signals so an electrical contractor ranks well on Google when a homeowner or business searches for electrical services. It covers four areas: a website that's fast, secure, mobile-friendly, and structured so Google can read it; an optimized GBP with the right categories and service areas; local citations and reviews that build prominence; and content that targets the specific homeowner questions that drive booked jobs — 'do I need a permit for a panel upgrade,' 'how much does an EV charger installation cost.'
How do I market myself as an electrician?
Honest answer for an IE electrician: stop spending on every channel at once. The order of impact is GBP optimization first (right categories, service area, photos, reviews), then a website Google can actually read and homeowners can read on their phone, then a steady review-request cadence after every install. Once those three are right, layer Google Local Service Ads for emergency-call keywords ('emergency electrician Redlands') where the homeowner can't wait three months for organic to move. Social and brochure mailers usually come last — they build awareness, not booked jobs.
How do electricians get clients from Google?
Most service calls come from Google's Maps 3-pack and Local Service Ads, not the blue-link organic results below. The 3-pack ranks on three signals: proximity to the searcher, relevance of your GBP categories and service areas, and prominence (reviews, citations, on-site authority). High-ticket work — panel upgrades, EV charger installations, whole-home rewires — also rewards informational content on your site, because those homeowners research for days before they call. The contractors winning in the IE are the ones whose GBP and their website are both pulling weight.
Should electricians run Google Ads (LSAs) or focus on SEO first?
Both, but in a specific order. SEO and GBP optimization compound — the work you do this month keeps paying in month 12. Google Local Service Ads and search ads only pay while the spend is on. For most IE electricians we recommend fixing the GBP and on-site basics first so the organic 3-pack and LSAs reinforce each other, then layering LSAs for emergency-call keywords. Spending on LSAs without a clean GBP underneath is paying retail for traffic Google would have sent you for free.
