## 4. §04 deep-dive narrative — the under-claimed-category story

### The visceral hook

Redlands is a city of ~70,000 people. We scanned a 5×5 grid (25 measurement points) around its downtown center for `roofer redlands`. **Across the whole grid, only four roofing GBPs are actively winning local-pack visibility.** Team All Star Construction (77 reviews, 100% SoLV), Roofing Alliance Inc (9 reviews, 100% SoLV), Hovey Roofing (8 reviews, 76% SoLV), and a tail. Google's index shows **32 different roofing GBPs categorized in the area**. Twenty-eight of them are invisible.

### The thesis

Roofing is the most structurally under-claimed local-services category in the Inland Empire we have measured:
- **Ontario** — 37 unique competitors, **6 active**
- **Fontana** — 47 unique competitors, **9 active**
- **San Bernardino** — 35 unique, **7 active**
- **Riverside** — 37 unique, **10 active**
- **Redlands** — 32 unique, **4 active**

### Why the category is under-claimed

Roofing in the Inland Empire is dominated by storm-driven demand, insurance-claim work, and a "storm-chaser" cohort of out-of-state operators who follow weather events. Operators in those modes do not invest in GBP optimization the way a steady-revenue trade does. **Saturated on paper, empty in the pack.**

### What an operator should do

A roofing operator who lands on this report and asks "what would it cost me to win the Redlands pack?" gets an honest answer: **collect 50-80 reviews, configure the GBP correctly, and you are competitive with Team All Star Construction inside two quarters.**

## 7. Industry-specific deep dive — IE roofing market characteristics

### Storm-season cycle
IE roof-replacement demand is driven by Santa Ana wind events (October-March), monsoon-season hail (July-September), and the Riverside/San Bernardino fire-rebuild market. Three demand spikes a year. A review-request system that **fires within 48 hours of job completion** captures the storm-season opportunity.

### Insurance-claim work distorts review behavior
Roofing review counts ceiling around 60-80 even at the top. Customer whose roof was covered by State Farm is not in the same review-leaving mindset as a customer who paid out-of-pocket. **Automated post-job review requests are mandatory.**

### "Storm-chaser" out-of-state operators
After major weather events, out-of-state operators flood the IE with door-to-door teams. These operators rarely build a local GBP. **The pack is not as crowded as the unique-GBP count suggests.**

### Residential vs. commercial split
The IE has a 70/30 residential/commercial split. Local-pack searches dominated by residential intent.

### What "good" looks like
**30-80 Google reviews** (not 300), a **GBP with `Roofing contractor` as the primary category**, NAP consistency across top-20 citation sites, automated post-job review requests.
