## 2. Search intent classification
- **Primary intent:** Commercial (0.532 on the search-intent endpoint for `window cleaning seo`, 0.634 for `seo for window cleaners`). On the DFS labs endpoint, the primary keyword reads as `informational` with `commercial` as foreign — researcher-leaning.
- **User goal in plain English:** A window-cleaning business owner (typically a 1-3 person operation, often a sole proprietor) is researching whether SEO is worth the spend. Window cleaning is a low-margin, low-ticket service vertical — owners are extremely price-sensitive and need to be reassured that a $1,000-2,000/month engagement makes ROI sense for a $150 average job.
- **Decision-stage:** Awareness-to-consideration — they're often still deciding *whether* SEO is worth it, not just *who*. Different from Plumbing/HVAC/Roofing where the buyer has already decided to spend.
- **Implication:** Page must teach more aggressively than the higher-volume industry pages (the buyer needs convincing SEO is worth it for window cleaning, not just that we're the right vendor). Lead with "is this even worth it for window cleaning?" framing.
