**The pattern:** in 4 of 5 IE cities, the pack leader has fewer Google reviews than the runner-up.

3. "Claim every category" doesn't mean "claim every category that contains your keywords"

### §07 — IE HVAC-specific market characteristics

**Heat-pump dominance shifting buyer journey.** Title 24, IRA rebates, SCE/SoCalGas rebate stacking.

**June-September emergency peak.** IE summer 100°F+ in interior cities. Searcher behavior overwhelmingly Maps-first.

**Multi-trade chains.** Ballard, Burgeson's, Sanborn's operate cross-trade. Pure-HVAC operators compete with operators having 3× review-generation surface area.

**Energy-rebate work changing highest-value job.** Whole-home heat pump replacements stacked with SCE TECH + federal 25C = $15k-$30k jobs with $4k-$8k stacked rebates.

### §08 — Reading this report alongside the flagship

The flagship found agency-pack victors with **0-1 reviews** holding 100% SoLV — won on GBP category cleanliness.

This HVAC report finds proximity + recent review velocity + GBP category cleanliness win even when competing GBPs have 5-10× more lifetime reviews.

**Common thread:** GBP category cleanliness is the foundation for both.

### §09 — Methodology

- **SoLV:** share of grid points where GBP appears in local pack, weighted by ranking position
- **Why `air conditioning service [city]`?** Returns trade contractors rather than HVAC-adjacent businesses
- **Why 5×5 grid?** Industry standard for small-to-mid IE cities
- **Why these 5 cities?** Cover IE pop distribution: ~1M residents combined
- **Limitations:** single-snapshot, single keyword (no `furnace`, `heat pump`-specific), 2-mi radius, residential intent
