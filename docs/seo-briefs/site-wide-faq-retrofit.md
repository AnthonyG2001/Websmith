### 2a. `index.html` — 6 Q&A

**Q1. Do I need local SEO if I drive to customers instead of having a storefront?**
Yes — possibly more than a storefront business does. Google calls you a "service-area business," and your Google Business Profile can hide its address while still ranking in the cities you serve. The catch: service-area businesses compete on the same Maps 3-pack as everyone else but without the proximity boost of a fixed location. That means reviews, GBP categories, and on-page service-area signals matter even more for you.

**Q2. Should I have a separate page for each city I serve?**
Generally yes, but only if each page actually says something different. Google penalizes near-duplicate "Plumber in [City]" pages where you swap one word. A page is worth building when it has the local context — neighborhoods you cover, jobs you've done in that area, the city-specific landmarks or HOAs your customers reference. Five honest city pages beat twenty thin ones. We build them only when there's real content to put on them.

**Q3. How is local SEO different from regular SEO?**
Regular SEO competes for the blue links anywhere in the country. Local SEO competes for the Maps 3-pack and the "near me" results inside your service area. The signals are different: regular SEO rewards backlinks and content depth; local SEO rewards Google Business Profile completeness, review count and recency, NAP consistency across directories, and geographic relevance. You can be strong at one and weak at the other. For a service business, local is what fills the calendar.

**Q4. What's the single biggest factor in ranking on Google Maps?** *(safer phrasing per AI CEO override)*
A complete, accurate Google Business Profile with the right primary category and service-area definition. Everything else — reviews, citations, on-page SEO — amplifies a GBP that's set up correctly, but no amount of amplification fixes a GBP missing the right category or pointing to a fake address. Step one of every engagement is auditing the GBP. When the GBP is the bottleneck, fixing it alone often moves rankings within 30 to 60 days.

**Q5. Can I do local SEO myself, or do I really need an agency?**
You can absolutely do the basics yourself: claim your GBP, pick the right primary category, ask every customer for a review, and keep your hours current. That's maybe 60% of the value. The remaining 40% — citations across the right directories, schema markup, geo-grid scanning to spot ranking weak zones, on-page optimization for service-area keywords — is where most owners run out of time. Hire an agency when the lost revenue from being slow is greater than the retainer.

**Q6. How long until I see results from local SEO?**
First measurable movement on Maps rankings typically shows in 30 to 60 days after GBP fixes and a review cadence start. Organic-search ranking improvements on competitive keywords take 90 to 180 days because Google needs to crawl, re-evaluate, and re-index your pages. We re-run the geo-grid scan every quarter so you can see the movement on the same map you started with. Anyone promising faster than that is either lying or about to use techniques that will hurt you later.

### 2b. `Services.html` — 6 Q&A

**Q1. How much does local SEO cost per month?**
The industry range is roughly $500 to $2,500 per month for a real retainer, with $1,000 to $1,500 being the most common service-business price point. One-time project audits run $500 to $5,000, and hourly consulting is typically $75 to $200. We publish both of our plan prices on this page rather than gating them behind a "request a quote" form. If your service area is unusually large or competitive, we'll tell you that before you sign anything.

**Q2. Why is your pricing different from other local SEO agencies?**
Two reasons. First, we're new — we're trading published, lower retainers for the chance to build a case-study roster over the next 12 months. Second, we work only with service-business owners in the Inland Empire, so we're not rebuilding the same playbook for every new industry. Our cost structure stays predictable, so yours does too. When the case-study roster is full, our pricing will reset to industry norms.

**Q3. What's actually included in the Standard plan vs. the Flagship plan?**
Standard covers the local-SEO playbook: Google Business Profile optimization, citations across the right directories, NAP consistency, monthly review-request workflows, on-page SEO on your existing site, and quarterly geo-grid scans so you can see ranking movement. Flagship adds a full custom website build plus the lead engine (forms, tracking, call routing). If your existing site is fine, Standard is the right starting point. If it's costing you trust on first impression, start with Flagship.

**Q4. Do you charge a setup fee?**
No setup fee. We charge a flat monthly retainer that starts the day work begins, and the first 90 days carry no commission on leads we generate so you can see the system produce before paying performance fees. Most agencies in this space layer a $500 to $1,000 one-time setup on top of the monthly — that's normal, we just chose not to do it. The trade-off: we run month-to-month, so if it isn't producing, you leave.

**Q5. Can I switch between plans or cancel without penalty?**
Yes. Plans are month-to-month with 30 days' notice. You can move from Standard up to Flagship at any time (the new plan starts the next billing cycle), and you can downgrade if Flagship's website build is finished and you no longer need the heavier engine. There's no cancellation penalty and no clawback of work already delivered. SEO compounds, so leaving at month three means walking away from the first month it actually pays off — we'll tell you that going in.

**Q6. What if I'm in a more competitive market than average?**
We'll say so before you sign. High-competition markets (legal, healthcare, large metro plumbing) genuinely cost more to win because the review counts and backlink profiles you're competing against are higher. For Inland Empire service trades, our Standard plan handles most service areas. If your specific city plus category combination needs more — a denser citation push, a heavier content cadence — we'll quote that as an add-on rather than rolling it into a generic "premium" tier you don't need.

### 2c. `Contact.html` — 5 Q&A

**Q1. Which cities do you actually serve outside of Redlands?**
We work with service-business owners across the Inland Empire: Redlands, San Bernardino, Riverside, Ontario, Fontana, Rancho Cucamonga, and roughly fifteen other cities in San Bernardino and Riverside counties. Our office is in Redlands. We don't take on clients outside the IE because local SEO benefits from knowing the neighborhoods, HOAs, and commuter patterns of the area you're competing in — and we'd rather be right about one region than vague about the country.

**Q2. How quickly will I hear back after I send the form or call?**
The same business day. Every inquiry — form submission, phone call, or email — gets a real human reply by end of business on the day we receive it. If you contact us over the weekend, you'll hear back the next business day. We don't use auto-responders or chatbots for the first reply. The person who reads your message is the person you'll be working with if we end up engaging.

**Q3. What's actually in the free 30-day GBP audit?**
A hand-written review of your Google Business Profile and the geo-grid around your service area. We pull a Local Falcon scan of your primary keyword, check your GBP categories, service-area definition, review velocity, photo recency, and citation consistency across the major directories. You get a written summary of what's working, what's broken, and what to fix first. No sales pitch wrapped around it — if the audit is enough for you to fix things yourself, that's a fine outcome.

**Q4. What happens after I submit the contact form?**
You get an email confirmation immediately, then a personal reply from one of us the same business day. The first reply usually asks a few clarifying questions (your service area, your top three competitor names, whether you already have a GBP claimed). From there we either book a 20-minute call or send the free audit directly, depending on what makes more sense. No automated funnel — we read every message ourselves.

**Q5. What should I have ready before I contact you?**
Three things help us reply with something useful instead of generic. One: your business name and the city your GBP is registered in, so we can pull your current rankings before we respond. Two: the service area you actually cover (some owners list a city but work three over). Three: whether you've worked with an SEO agency before and what went wrong if so. None of these are required — you can also just send a URL and we'll go from there.
