What ranking an HVAC contractor actually takes

HVAC SEO isn't a content-marketing problem. It's a local-search problem. Three things move the needle — done well, every month.

  • A Google Business Profile that shows up in the map. The right Google categories for your trade. Service area cleaned up. Reviews requested after every install and service call. Posts that match what homeowners actually search — "AC won't turn on," "furnace blowing cold," not "comfort solutions."
  • A website Google can read. The technical SEO setup so Google understands what you sell. Service-area pages for the cities you actually drive to, not a fake nationwide footprint. Fast page load. One homeowner question per page — no "comfort experts since 1987" filler that ranks for nothing.
  • Reviews and listings, on schedule. Your business name, address, and phone number stay consistent across the trade directories Google checks. Reviews requested regularly — we time the ask to the moment the customer is most likely to leave one.

[05]  QUESTIONS HVAC OWNERS ASK

HVAC SEO, answered straight.

How do I promote my HVAC business online?
Three things, in order of impact: a fully optimized Google Business Profile with the right categories (HVAC Contractor, Furnace Repair Service, AC Repair Service, Heating Contractor — claim every one that applies); a website Google can read (schema markup, fast LCP, one homeowner question per page); and a steady review-request cadence after every install and service call. Homeowners search Google the moment the AC quits — the contractors in the 3-pack get the call.
How do HVAC contractors get more customers from Google?
Most service calls come from Google's Maps 3-pack, not the blue-link results below it. The 3-pack ranks on three signals: proximity to the searcher, relevance of your GBP categories and service areas, and prominence (reviews, citations, on-site authority). Local SEO addresses all three. If your competitors are in the 3-pack for 'AC repair near me' and you're not, you're effectively invisible to a homeowner staring at a thermostat that won't drop below 84.
Should HVAC contractors run Google Ads or focus on SEO?
Both, but in a specific order. SEO and GBP optimization compound — the work you do this month keeps paying in month 12. Google Local Service Ads (LSAs) and search ads pay only while the spend is on. For most IE HVAC contractors we recommend fixing the GBP and on-site basics first so the organic 3-pack and LSAs reinforce each other, then layering LSAs for emergency-call keywords ('emergency AC repair Redlands') where the homeowner can't wait three months for an organic ranking to move.
How long does HVAC SEO take to work?
First measurable movement on Maps rankings typically shows in 30 to 60 days after GBP fixes and review cadence start. Organic-search ranking improvements on competitive keywords like 'AC repair [city]' take 90 to 180 days because Google needs to crawl, re-evaluate, and re-index your pages. We re-run the geo-grid scan every quarter so you see the movement on the same map you started with — not on a vanity rank-tracker that cherry-picks queries.
