## 4. §04 deep-dive — "The proximity-rewards-recency thesis"

In 4 of 5 IE cities scanned for `restaurants [city]` on 2026-05-13, **a sub-300-review restaurant won or tied the local pack** while one or more multi-thousand-review competitors sat at 0-8% SoLV.

**The starkest case is San Bernardino.** Pack leader: Cheliz Restaurant, 94 reviews, 92% SoLV. Sitting at 0-8% SoLV in the same grid:
- BJ's Restaurant & Brewhouse — **3,324 reviews**
- Sundowners — **3,732 reviews**
- Mexico Cafe — **3,156 reviews**
- Claim Jumper — **2,935 reviews**
- Mimi's Cafe — **2,054 reviews**
- Mitla Cafe — **2,054 reviews**
- Tony's Italian — **1,861 reviews**

Seven heritage restaurants, 19,116 combined reviews — Google's local pack routes none of that visibility to them. (Source: LF scan `50b2ecc91a677ae`.)

Pattern repeats with smaller magnitudes in 3 other cities:
- **Ontario:** El Balcon Rooftop wins with **29 reviews** at 76% SoLV. Tofu Chon (420 reviews) sits at 40%. (Scan `b034cc305caf4e2`.)
- **Riverside:** The Outpost wins with **148 reviews** at 92% SoLV. Tio's Tacos (5,135 reviews) and Old Spaghetti Factory (4,726 reviews) both sit at 0%. (Scan `f1bbaaf42fa2724`.)
- **Fontana:** Dave's Hot Chicken with **3,934 reviews** ranks only 3rd behind two ~220-review locals. (Scan `06bfef3dad247a0`.)

Only **Redlands** shows alignment between review-count and rank: The State (1,530, 100%), Finney's (581, 96%), Tartan (1,479, 76%). (Scan `8edd88e868001e8`.)

### Why heritage isn't helping

1. **Proximity-to-grid-center dominates the IE restaurant pack.** A restaurant 0.3 miles from the dense downtown core consistently outranks one 1.5 miles out, regardless of review count.
2. **Photo recency and posting cadence beat review recency.** Most 0%-SoLV heritage spots have photo streams that haven't been refreshed since 2022.
3. **Review-count saturation has diminishing returns past ~500.**

### Practical implication

If you are a new or sub-500-review restaurant in the IE — particularly in San Bernardino, Riverside, Ontario, or Fontana — the local pack is **more open than your competitors' review counts suggest**. The work isn't "match Tio's Tacos at 5,135 reviews." It's clean GBP categories, monthly photo refresh, posts matching diner search language, and 50-200 reviews built with real cadence.

**Redlands is the exception, not the rule.**

### The Ontario El Balcon vs. Tofu Chon mini-case

Ontario has 28 active restaurants out of 96 unique. El Balcon Rooftop (29 reviews) holds 76% SoLV. Tofu Chon (420 reviews) holds 40%. 14× the review base for half the visibility.

What El Balcon has that Tofu Chon doesn't (public GBP inspection 2026-05-13):
- "Trendy rooftop" category cleanly set + Ontario downtown-dining categories
- Photo stream refreshed within past 14 days
- Posts about active happy-hour pricing within past 30 days
- 5-star avg rating across 29 reviews (Tofu Chon 420 reviews averages 4.3 stars)

**Category cleanliness + freshness producing 2× pack visibility despite 14× review gap.**

### Cross-reference: inverse of agency-pack pattern

Our [2026 IE Local SEO Report](IELocalSEOReport.html) §4 found agency pack rankings weak on review depth (no agency has built a meaningful review base). Restaurant pack has the same outcome (review-count weaker than expected) but for a different reason: review counts exist (and are huge), but Google weights freshness over depth.

## 7. §07 — IE restaurant market characteristics

### 1. Cuisine clustering — Mexican concentration
Mexican and Mexican-American make up the plurality of unique GBPs in 4 of 5 cities (SB 22 of 75, Fontana 31 of 113, Ontario 24 of 96, Riverside 18 of 68; Redlands diversified). Within cuisine cluster, freshness signals dominate.

### 2. Chain dominance ceiling
- Dave's Hot Chicken in Fontana: 3,934 reviews, ranks #3
- BJ's Brewhouse in SB: 3,324 reviews, ≤8% SoLV
- Claim Jumper, Mimi's Cafe, Old Spaghetti Factory: all 0% SoLV across multiple cities

The IE restaurant pack penalizes centrally-managed GBPs in favor of single-location owner-operators.

### 3. Dine-in vs takeout split since 2020
Post-2020, IE diner search patterns shifted toward `[cuisine] near me`, `[neighborhood] lunch`, `happy hour [city]` — away from named-brand searches. Multi-category GBPs appear in 3-4 more diner-search permutations.

### 4. Photo-pack signals as ranking factor
Restaurants have a Google-specific surface other verticals don't: the photo pack inside Maps. Every #1 in our 5 cities has photos uploaded within past 60 days. 0%-SoLV heritage spots typically don't.

### How these factors compound

A new IE restaurant doing the work — clean multi-category GBP, monthly photo refresh, occasion-keyword posting, 50-200 review base — can credibly outrank a decade-old 3,000-review spot. **The barrier is operational discipline, not review-count history.**
