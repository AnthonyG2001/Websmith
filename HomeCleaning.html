What ranking a cleaning business actually takes

Cleaning SEO isn't a content-marketing problem. It's a local-pack problem with the strongest review-velocity advantage of any service vertical. Three things move that needle.

  • GBP that wins the 3-pack. The right categories audited (House Cleaning Service, Maid Service, and Carpet Cleaning Service where applicable). Service area drawn to your actual cleaner-deployment radius — not a 25-mile vanity radius that burns drive time. Reviews requested after every cleaning, while the customer is still in the freshly-cleaned space. Posts that match what homeowners actually search — "move-out cleaning quote," "deep clean before guests," "recurring weekly cleaning" — not "spotless guarantee."
  • A website Google can read. Schema markup for HouseCleaningService + Service + AggregateOffer where applicable. Quote-request workflow that captures square footage, bedrooms/bathrooms, and recurring vs. one-time up front so you can quote accurately without a site visit. Page speed under 2.5s LCP. Photo galleries of before/after that load lazy — these convert, but only if they load instantly.
  • Reviews & local citations on schedule. NAP consistency on the directories Google checks for cleaning services (BBB, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack, Yelp, Nextdoor, Care.com for residential). Review cadence timed to immediate post-clean text, not next-week email — same-day asks convert at meaningfully higher rates for cleaning specifically (the customer is sitting in the result the moment they decide to review).

[05]  QUESTIONS CLEANING OWNERS ASK

Home cleaning SEO, answered straight.

How do I market my cleaning business?
Three things, in order of impact: a fully optimized Google Business Profile with the right categories (House Cleaning Service or Maid Service for residential, Carpet Cleaning Service if applicable, Commercial Cleaning Service for janitorial accounts) and a service area drawn to your actual cleaner-deployment radius; a website Google can read (schema markup, fast LCP, pages for the services you actually offer like move-out cleans and recurring weekly); and a steady review-request cadence the moment the clean is finished. Residential cleaning has the strongest same-day review-conversion rate of any service vertical we work with — the customer is sitting in the result.
Is SEO worth it for a small cleaning business?
For most Inland Empire cleaning operators, yes — the recurring-revenue math is favorable. A residential weekly or bi-weekly customer is typically worth $120 to $300 per month for years if you don't churn them. If a $1,200 monthly SEO investment adds 6 to 10 new recurring customers per year, the lifetime value crushes the spend. If your average customer churns under 4 months or your cleaner roster is already over-booked, the math shifts. We'll tell you that before you sign anything.
Should I focus on residential or commercial cleaning SEO?
Pick one as the primary on your website and footnote the other. Residential and commercial cleaning are different markets with different search behavior — residential buyers search 'house cleaning near me' from mobile and book in 24-48 hours; commercial buyers search 'commercial janitorial Riverside' from desktop and may take 30-60 days to sign a contract. A page that tries to be both at once tends to rank for neither. If you do both genuinely, build a primary page for the dominant revenue source and a secondary page for the other. We'll help you pick which is which during the diagnostic.
How long does cleaning business SEO take to work?
First measurable movement on Maps rankings typically shows in 30 to 60 days after GBP fixes and review cadence start. Organic-search ranking improvements on keywords like 'house cleaning [city]' take 90 to 180 days because Google needs to crawl, re-evaluate, and re-index your pages. Cleaning has one advantage other home-service verticals don't: review velocity. A residential cleaning business doing 10 cleanings a day can build a stronger review base in 3 months than a roofer can in 12 — and reviews are a major ranking factor. We re-run the geo-grid scan every quarter so you see the movement on the same map you started with.
Do you require a 12-month contract?
No. We run month-to-month. If the work isn't producing, we'd rather you leave than be locked in resenting us. The trade-off is honest: SEO compounds, so leaving at month 3 means walking away from the first month it actually starts paying off. We tell you that going in.
